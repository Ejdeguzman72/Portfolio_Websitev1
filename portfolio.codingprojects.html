Ethics in Information Systems (CIS150)

This course introduced how ethics and information security play hand in hand. Over the duration of this course, students are exposed to the different areas of ethics that can play in an organiation in regards to information systems. From that information, students are expected to come up with solutions to these problems in the form of case reports.

Introduction to Database Administration (CIS310)

CIS310 introduced into database design and implementation in various coursework over the duration of the course. One of the most important design specifications comes from the Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) as it is an outline of the database. Once students are comfortable with the design aspect of database administration, students engage in the use of Structured Query Language (SQL). Areas that are covered are the use of queries, trigger, database creation, and stored procedure.



System Design and Analysis (CIS320)

CIS320 exposed students in working with a client so that students can understand how to identify business needs and think of solutions in order to resolve the business problems that the organization is facing. As the course went on, students worked in groups designing the layout of the system and presenting it to the client. In designing the system, students identified system requirements in order to narrow a direction in which the design specifications can go. Useful tools associated with the design process were use cases, entity relationship diagram. data dictionary, class diagram, navigation diagrams, and the economic feasibility. The final product was the vision document that is a report that summarizes all the deliverables made throughout the course. These deliverables were presented to the client after each iteration.



Management of Infomration Systems (CIS410)

CIS410 involved students in analyzing various types of businesses in case reports. Each case report outlined the problems that the organization was facing. From the infomration that was gathered from analyzing the case reports, students were expected to propose solutions which were conducted in the form of reports. Student were also expected to conduct a strategic assessment of an organization's IT architecture of their own choosing. The strategic assessment consisted of the organizations's current system in regards to its strengths and weaknesses, offering ways that it can further improve upon it.
